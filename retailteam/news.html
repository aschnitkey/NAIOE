NAI Ohio Equities gets job to find retailers for New Albany

May 20, 2016

The ever-growing New Albany International Business Park and its more than 13,000 workers need places to eat and shop, and it will be up to to reel them in.

New Albany Co., the meticulous master planner of the eponymous suburb and its sprawling business park, has taken the unusual step of bringing in an outside brokerage to market 42 acres set aside for retailing and consumer services.

“Historically we’ve done them ourselves,” New Albany Co. President William Ebbing said, noting the company attracted Hampton Inn and Marriott Courtyard hotels, several restaurants and a Turkey Hill service station to the area. “We have an awful lot of great new retailers in place, but we think it’s now time to bring in a team of experts who really specialize in this area. We consider them an extension of our team.”

The NAI Ohio Equities agents handling the sites are Anthony Maronitis and Chris Howard.

Sullivan Bruck Gets New Digs

July 31, 2015

Philadelphia-based Campus Apartments Inc in 2008 renovated the historic 10-story building, once home to the Seneca Hotel, into an apartment complex. The 77 apartments are targeted largely toward students attending colleges downtown. Sullivan Bruck, a design firm leaving the Mercantile Building downtown later this year, will lease the ballroom floor and become the building’s first office tenant since 1987, when the Ohio Environmental Protection Agency vacated it after operating there for 15 years.

LaRosa's coming to Central Ohio with Dublin pizzeria

April 22, 2014

The LaRosa's Family Pizzeria family soon will include Central Ohio.

The 60-year-old Cincinnati institution has secured Brandon Salamone as its first area franchisee and 7048 Hospital Drive in Dublin as its first pizzeria here.

"I didn't feel like I had a choice," Salamone joked. "When you grow up in Cincinnati, (LaRosa's) is a part of life, like the Reds, the Bengals, Graeter's Ice Cream, Skyline Chili. They had my order on stand-by."
